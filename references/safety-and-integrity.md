# Integrity Guidance For Detector Mentions

Use this file only when the user mentions AI detectors, humanizing, detector evasion, Turnitin, GPTZero, Originality.ai, Winston, ZeroGPT, or similar tools.

## Boundary

Do not help the user bypass a detector. Do not promise a score. Do not optimize a rewrite for evasion. Do not add fake errors, random slang, invisible characters, Unicode substitutions, spurious citations, invented drafts, or fabricated process history.

Redirect to transparent editing:

- Make claims more specific.
- Preserve the author's actual meaning and stance.
- Remove unsupported authority claims.
- Improve paragraph movement.
- Define terms the intended reader needs.
- Keep citations and quotations accurate.
- Preserve the register required by the assignment, venue, or institution.

## How To Respond

If the user asks for bypass:

```text
I cannot help evade detection or promise a detector result. I can edit the text so the argument is clearer, more specific, and easier to defend as your own work.
```

Then proceed with the edit if the underlying request is legitimate.

## What To Fix Instead

Detector complaints often correlate with real prose issues:

- Repetitive structure.
- Generic transitions.
- Vague claims.
- Missing evidence.
- Over-polished summary paragraphs.
- Decorative vocabulary.
- Sentence-by-sentence paraphrase that preserves the old shape.

Treat those as writing problems, not evasion targets.

## Academic And Institutional Work

For school, university, grant, legal, or workplace writing:

- Keep the writer's actual sources.
- Do not invent personal anecdotes.
- Do not invent research process.
- Do not hide AI assistance when disclosure is required.
- Prefer a neutral clarity pass over a personality-forward transformation.
