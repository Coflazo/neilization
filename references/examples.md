# Examples

Use these constructed examples to calibrate the transformation. They are not quotations from Neil deGrasse Tyson or any source text.

## Popular Science

**Before**

```text
Machine learning is a transformative technology that leverages data to identify patterns and make predictions across many industries.
```

**After**

```text
Machine learning begins with examples. Feed a model enough labeled X-rays, loan applications, or weather readings, and it adjusts its internal settings until its guesses stop being terrible. The machine has not acquired wisdom. It has fit a function to a mountain of cases. What changed in the past decade is the size of the mountain.
```

Why it works:

- Replaces hype with mechanism.
- Uses concrete examples.
- Keeps the claim bounded.
- Ends on scale, not slogan.

## Civic Cosmic Perspective

**Before**

```text
Public debates often become divided because people hold different beliefs and values. A more scientific mindset could help society become more rational.
```

**After**

```text
Most public arguments begin with people treating their beliefs as facts. Science asks for a colder sorting process. What was measured? What was inferred? What is merely preferred? Once those piles are separated, some disputes shrink. Others survive, but at least we know what we are actually arguing about.
```

Why it works:

- Sorts claims by evidence type.
- Avoids scolding.
- Returns the abstract point to public consequence.

## Formal Research Clarity

**Before**

```text
This paper provides a comprehensive analysis of the effect of the policy, highlighting important implications for future research and decision-making.
```

**After**

```text
This paper estimates the policy's effect and reports where the estimate is stable, where it weakens, and which assumptions drive the result.
```

Why it works:

- Cuts promotional language.
- Names what the paper does.
- Keeps the register sober.

## Speech Or Interview

**Before**

```text
Scientists should engage in outreach because it is important for society and helps communicate science to the public.
```

**After**

```text
If the public funds your laboratory, the public deserves a bridge back to what you found there. Outreach is not decoration around the science. It is part of the social contract that lets the science exist.
```

Why it works:

- Answers directly.
- Uses a concrete institutional relationship.
- Widens to consequence without drifting into slogan.
