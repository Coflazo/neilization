# Structural Patterns

Use this file when the rewrite still feels generic, padded, or mechanical after the main voice pass.

## 1. Sentence-by-Sentence Paraphrase

Problem: the rewrite keeps the old sentence order and merely swaps words.

Fix: redraft by paragraph job. Ask what each paragraph must do: define, prove, contrast, scale, humanize, or conclude. Then write fresh paragraphs around those jobs.

## 2. Setup-Payoff Repetition

Problem: every paragraph opens with a short claim and follows with a longer explanation.

Fix: vary movement. Sometimes begin with the example, sometimes the measurement, sometimes the misconception, sometimes the consequence.

## 3. Decorative Triples

Problem: repeated "X, Y, and Z" lists make prose sound packaged.

Fix: use the number the material actually supports. Two can create contrast. Four can map a system. One strong example can beat a list.

## 4. Summary Closers

Problem: the paragraph's final sentence restates the opening.

Fix: close on a consequence, image, number, contrast, or next question. If the final sentence adds nothing, delete it.

## 5. Generic Funnel Opening

Problem: the piece opens with a broad universal claim before reaching the topic.

Fix: open with a date, scene, measurement, object, public habit, or concrete claim. Let the broad meaning arrive later.

## 6. Over-Explained Definitions

Problem: the prose teaches every term as if the reader knows nothing.

Fix: define only the terms required for the intended reader. Use short inline definitions. For specialist register, keep field terms and trust the audience.

## 7. Vantage Drift

Problem: the rewrite invokes cosmic perspective but never returns to the user's topic.

Fix: every scale shift must come back to the human consequence. Orbit, deep time, or alien observers are lenses, not destinations.

## 8. Unsupported Upgrades

Problem: the rewrite adds dates, statistics, names, or anecdotes because specificity sounds better.

Fix: if the source does not support the detail and it is not safe general knowledge, remove it or mark it as a question for the user.

## 9. Personality Overload

Problem: every paragraph contains a joke, cosmic analogy, or rhetorical flourish.

Fix: make most sentences do plain explanatory work. Use one memorable flourish only where it clarifies the thought.

## 10. Argument Without Pressure

Problem: paragraphs can be rearranged without changing the argument.

Fix: create dependencies. A claim should invite evidence; evidence should force a consequence; a consequence should motivate the next paragraph.

## Audit Steps

1. Name each paragraph's job in the margin.
2. Delete any paragraph whose job duplicates the previous one.
3. Underline every number and decide whether it needs translation.
4. Circle every abstract noun and ask what concrete thing it refers to.
5. Mark every scale shift and verify it returns to the topic.
6. Read the final sentence of each paragraph. If it restates, cut or replace it.
